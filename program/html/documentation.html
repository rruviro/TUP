Technological University of the Philippines Documentation

History of Technological University of the Philippines

The Technological University of the Philippines (TUP) was founded in 1974 as a state university. It was established with the mission to provide affordable, high-quality education in engineering, industrial technology, and related fields to Filipinos. TUP has since grown into one of the leading centers for technical education and research in the country.

TUP History

MTS Era (1901–1910)

  • Originally established as the Manila Trade School (MTS).
  • Purpose: Provide practical instruction in various trades.
  • Courses: Carpentry, English, telegraphy, and more.

PSAT Era (1910–1959)

  • Renamed as Philippine School of Arts and Trades (PSAT).
  • Developed technical courses and introduced secondary-level education.

PCAT Era (1959–1978)

  • Became Philippine College of Arts and Trades (PCAT).
  • Offered graduate degrees and enhanced technical programs.

TUP Era (1978–Present)

  • Elevated to Technological University of the Philippines (TUP).
  • Focus: Advanced vocational and technical education.
